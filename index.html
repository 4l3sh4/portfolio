The Funny Yet Concerning Adventures of Kippi the Cat

Python • Feb 2024


A Python-only text-based adventure game that I made for a Problem Solving and Program Design assignment during the second semester of my foundation year. It is heavily inspired by the classic Japanese role-playing games made in the 90s, such as Dragon Quest, Final Fantasy and Pokémon.

This project utilizes a lot of lists and dictionaries for its inventory management system, functions that are used for player and enemy attacks, if-else statements for the item drops and character dialogue choices, as well as a save file function so that the player can continue their journey if they were to ever take a break from playing the game. It consists of 6,508 lines of code, 4 areas that the player can traverse throughout their journey, 21 enemies, 4 mini-bosses, and one final boss fight. Each enemy has their own unique attacks, and stat distribution.

Not only that, this Python game also contains 25 different armour that improve the user's stats, 22 consumables that give various effects— good and bad— to the player, and 25 weapons, which totals up to 72 different items that the player can collect within a play-through. The game's ending also changes based on the dialogue choices that the player has made when playing the game, giving it a little bit of replayability.

Looking back, this was quite the daunting project, but I had lots of fun with it! I'd definitely try to re-create a project like this whenever I have the free time to do so.
